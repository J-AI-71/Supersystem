Tracking links explained

Tracking parameters are extra bits attached to a link — like labels on a package. They can help platforms measure campaigns, but when you forward the link they often become unnecessary ballast.

What these “labels” look like

https://example.com/article?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=launch
https://shop.example.com/product?gclid=ABC123&fbclid=XYZ987

utm_* campaign tags

Useful for analytics — often unnecessary when forwarding.

gclid / fbclid click IDs

Platform-specific IDs used for measurement — usually ballast when sharing.

When you should NOT remove everything blindly

Affiliate / partner attribution

Some parameters are intended attribution (e.g. ref, tag). Removing them can break attribution.

Pro: allowlists/policies per domain.

Functional parameters

Some keys are needed (language, product variant, filters, session). A good cleaner removes typical tracking but avoids random deletions.

App: standard tracking keys. Pro: audit + rules.

No “invisible mode” promise: reducing tracking labels is one concrete step — not total anonymity.
