Tracking parameters are extra bits of data attached to links — like labels on a package. They can be useful for analytics, but when you forward/share, they often become unnecessary baggage.

What do “labels” look like?

https://example.com/article?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=launch
https://shop.example.com/product?gclid=ABC123&fbclid=XYZ987
utm_* campaigns

Shows source/medium/campaign. Useful for reports — often unnecessary when sharing.

gclid / fbclid click IDs

Platform-specific IDs used for measurement. Usually baggage when you forward/share.

Why should non-professionals care?

  • Respect: You pass along less extra data that others didn’t choose.
  • Clarity: Clean links look more trustworthy and less “ad-like”.
  • Fewer breaks: Some apps shorten/modify long URLs in ways that can break them.
Important: this is not an “invisible” promise. It’s a concrete hygiene step when sharing links.

When you should NOT remove everything

Affiliate / partner attribution

Some parameters are intentional attribution (e.g. ref, tag). Removing them can break commission or attribution.

Pro: allowlists/policies per domain.

Functional parameters

Some keys are required (language, product IDs, sessions, filters). Good tools are conservative and don’t remove everything blindly.

App: standard tracking. Pro: audit + rules.
